Some Background On Me

From the time I was a little girl, I begged my parents to put me in piano lessons. I wanted to be a concert pianist, like Lang-Lang and so many of my other childhood idols. I started taking lessons when I was six, and had a sharp ear for pitch and a quick memory when it came to memorizing sheet music. I enrolled in the Certificate of Merit program with the Music Teacher's Association of California, regularly participating and winning classical competitions as well as completing and passing the yearly evaluations, which included sight-reading, ear training, technique, advanced music theory, repertoire preparation and memorization, finishing the highest level in my sophomore year of high school. Afterwards, I needed to take a break from classical pieces and instead focused on fun music; movie themes, contemporary, and jazz. These methods helped me in every genre I learned, and I hope they can help you too!

My Five Tips

  1. Read over the sheet music
  2. Practice sight-reading
    • Use audial resources (like pre-played recordings of the song) to learn quicker
    • Practice left-hand and right-hand separately
    • Use a metronome
  3. Mark the sheet music while practicing to record personalized notes
  4. Practice without looking at each measure
  5. Put sheet music on the floor and only glance if needed
  6. Practice the music without any sheet music at all!
